Hugo Aguiar

Attorney | LL.M. Harvard Law School | AI & Energy Governance Consultant

Hugo Aguiar is an Ecuadorian attorney and consultant specializing in energy governance, natural resource policy, public law, and AI regulation. He holds an LL.M. from Harvard Law School, where he was awarded the Dean's Scholar Prize (2017), and an MBA.

He has held senior positions at the intersection of energy governance and public policy, including as Presidential Advisor to the President of Ecuador (2013–2015 and 2021–2022), Senior Consultant at the World Bank Group (2017–2022), Vice Minister of Oil and Gas (2022), Minister of Energy of Ecuador (2022), and General Manager of EP Petroecuador (2022–2023). He is currently a Consultant at Zentrum Atlas in the New York City area.

His research focuses on sovereign energy contract architecture, rent capture in oil-exporting countries, and the regulatory implications of artificial intelligence across affected industries.
